This Spyral Notebook has been created for a Digital Humanities Project at Durham University. The project aims to analyses female funerayr stelae to understand the extent of gender bias in the ancient world. This spyral Notebook presents two datasets 1. female epigrapghy 2. male epighrapghy. The viewer can use the corpus to further understand such themes of gender bias. 

The viewer can infer that though both the male and female corpus have a similar amount of noted inscriptions each, there is less words on average used in the female corpus. furthermore most popular words for women are "of" or "daughter" typically of does not seem to be an important word however, of is used to demonstarted who ro where a woman belongs to. yet for male epigrapghy from the same data set has significantly more words with highlights of words such as "son" or "excellence" showing up as well as "tribe" due to the nature of one of the funerary epitaphs being a war monument. yet, male epigraphy shows far more evidence of descriptive mention sof the persons character or life compare to a females. 

A gap in the research arose from this epigraphy analysis. Monument iconography must be analysed or this project would be weakly supported and would have not taken adavtnage of such a small get rich dataset. Iconograpghy analysis is completed in recogito anf listed in the repository.
